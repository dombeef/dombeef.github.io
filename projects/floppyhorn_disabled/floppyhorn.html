Floppy Unicorn Horn Tutorial

Project Description

Since this project has become a bit of a popular hit, I decided to show how it was done, and how anyone can create their own "jiggly" models for their own creative snapchat lenses!


Tools needed

- A Mac or Windows computer

- Snapchat Lens Studio(The latest version being v2.3 at the time of writing)

- Blender 2.79(Any other version will work, but the interface will not be the same as shown below)

- Unicorn horn 3d model or any other 3d model you want to use(Assuming it isn't too complex and has less than 65k polygons, preferably far less)


Step 1 - Open Blender

- Using the "unicorn_horn.blend" file that you downloaded earlier, double click on it to open it up within Blender.

Step 2 - Add Bone

- Press Shift + A to activate the Add keyboard shortcut. Once that window pops up, point to the Armature and then click on the Single Bone button as shown above. It's okay if you can't see this bone immedately, later on I'll show how to make it visible and adjust it's location.

Step 3 - Enable X-Ray mode to see all bones

- Now on your numpad press 5(The hotkey to switch between perspective/orthographic view), and then 1(The hotkey to align your screen along the xz plane) to position your screen as shown above.

If you are not perfectly in focus of your 3d object, you can use your scroll wheel adjust your view. Scrolling on the scroll wheel will let you zoom in and out on your scene. Clicking down and holding your scroll wheel button will let you rotate your perspective around the center of your screen. Holding down the Shift key and then holding down on the scroll wheel key will allow you to move your perspective around the scene.
